Critical Insights

The Outdoor Instinct

Running Outside During an Earthquake

72% of earthquake injuries in urban environments occur outdoors — caused by falling glass, facade debris, and signage. The impulse to run outside is the single deadliest earthquake instinct.

Correct action: Drop → Cover → Hold On

Source: USGS / California Seismic Safety Commission

The Rule of Threes

Human Survival Thresholds

Without air3 minutes
Without shelter (extreme cold)3 hours
Without water3 days
Without food3 weeks
Shelter > Water > Food — in that order

Source: US Army Survival Manual FM 21-76

Smoke Alarm Effect

The Single Biggest Fire Lifesaver

Working smoke alarms reduce the risk of dying in a home fire by 55%. Yet 3 out of 5 home fire deaths occur in buildings with no working alarm — either absent or with dead batteries.

You have ~3 min to escape before flashover

Source: NFPA Fire Loss Report 2022
